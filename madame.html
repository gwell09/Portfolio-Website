Madame Vita's Fortunes
Fall 2023 | Unity
Madame Vita's Fortunes is a tarot-like game that has an early twenties carnival theme. You play as a character that enters Madame Vita's tent to recieve a tarot reading. The rules are explained, you choose cards from the deck, and recieve your fortune.

Ideation

This was a prototype I made for a learning exercise based on a "Stacking" theme. I followed this theme by having a stack of tarot cards in the game.

This game is different from my other games because of the aesthetic, which was very gothic and sultry. I also don't do many 2D games and I utilized a new drawing style when creating the sprite of Madame Vita. Below are some snippits of code, documentation of my ideation process, a reference.

Snippits of code for the card randomization

Emotions

For this game, I envisioned a very sultry, mysterious vibe. I created this kind of environment by taking a lot of inspiration from the 20s. I used royalty free 20s ragtime music and images from carnivals in the early 1900s. I also used fonts and backround patterns that are a little more gothic as well. In addition to all of this, I also made Madame Vita take after a flapper.

A few of the tarot cards the player can receive

Timeline

The timeline for this game was one week

For each of my one week prototypes, the timeline typically includes ideation, greyboxing, environmental design, and delivery. However, since the timeline is so short, the number of days I work and the day each phase is assigned to varies.

  

10/7: Ideation, General game mechanics layout

10/8: Greyboxing, Programming

10/9: Writing fortunes, Drawing sprites, final touches

10/10: Game due

Responsibilities

Team size: 1 (solo project)

All assets except the sprite of Madame Vita are not my own

 

- Utilizing Yarn Spinner for the dialogue system

- Drawing the Madame Vita sprite in Procreate and designing cards

- Programming card randomization mechanic, making card readings active when the player pressed on them

- Creating animations in Unity

- Writing fortunes and dialogue

Playtest Notes

In the first iteration of the game before I cleaned it up a little, there was a button to the left of the stack, which the player would press to choose their cards. The playtesters thought they were supposed to press the card stack since it said to "choose" them. This was a small change I made afterwards: making the stack itself the button to draw cards.

 

Challenges

The most difficult part of this game was the amount of writing I had to do for the fortunes since I don't write much. The pure volume of writing was enjoyable, but took much more time than I expected.
