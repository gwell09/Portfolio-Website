Madame Vita's Fortunes
Madame Vita's Fortunes
2023 | Unity
Madame Vita knows more than just your future...flip the cards and discover your fate.
Step into the flickering glow of a 1920s carnival, where fate is just a card flip away. In this one-week prototype, you enter the mysterious tent of Madame Vita, a fortune teller whose readings are never quite what they seem. Choose your cards, uncover cryptic messages, and watch as your destiny unfolds. Will you embrace the fortune you’re given, or attempt to change what’s already been written in the stars?
  
  

Ideation

I designed this one week prototype to have fun with a very gothic, sultry aesthetic and to hone my illustration and writing skills. Based on the theme "Stacking," I decided to create a game centered around a fortune teller and her mysterious stack of cards.

Emotions

For this game, I envisioned a very sultry, mysterious vibe. I created this kind of environment by taking a lot of inspiration from the 20s. I used royalty-free 20s ragtime music and images from carnivals in the early 1900s. I also used fonts and background patterns that are a little more gothic as well. In addition to all of this, I also made Madame Vita take after a flapper.

A few of the tarot cards the player can receive

Timeline: 1 Week

For each of my one week prototypes, the timeline typically includes ideation, greyboxing, environmental design, and delivery. However, since the timeline is so short, the number of days I work and the day each phase is assigned to varies.

10/7: Ideation, General game mechanics layout

10/8: Greyboxing, Programming

10/9: Writing fortunes, Drawing sprites, final touches

10/10: Game due

Responsibilities

Team size: 1 (solo project). All assets except the sprite of Madame Vita are not my own.

- Utilizing Yarn Spinner for the dialogue system

- Drawing the Madame Vita sprite in Procreate and designing cards

- Programming card randomization mechanic, making card readings active when the player pressed on them

- Creating animations in Unity

- Writing fortunes and dialogue

Playtest Notes

In the first iteration of the game, before I cleaned it up a little, there was a button to the left of the stack, which the player would press to choose their cards. The playtesters thought they were supposed to press the card stack since it said to "choose" them. This was a small change I made afterward: making the stack itself the button to draw cards.

Challenges

The most difficult part of this game was the amount of writing I had to do for the fortunes since I don't write much. The pure volume of writing was enjoyable but took much more time than I expected.
