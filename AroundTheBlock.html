A Walk Down the Block
A Walk Down the Block
2024 | Unity
In this city, the only thing bigger than the parks are the diapers.
A Walk Down the Block is a whimsical third-person adventure where you play as a baby in a city built entirely for babies. Parks are oversized playpens, sidewalks are cushioned, and cafes serve nothing but milky delights. Inspired by a long stroll along a canal in Romainville, Paris, this game explores how public and private spaces shape our interactions.
  
  

Ideation

I wanted to go in a more playful direction with this game, so I decided to equate the differences between public and private spaces with adult and children's spaces. I started by organizing the most notable aspects of each group before moving forward with greyboxing, writing, etc. Many of these aspects are implemented through environmental design and audio. I also put together some reference games, which are all walking simulators.

I was inspired by walking simulators like Babbdi, Baby Walking Simulator, and Fish Market.

Emotions

Ultimately, this is a funny, playful exploration game. Because the topic of design for the public can be very serious in some cases, I wanted to make the topic easier to digest by taking an approach, keeping humor and joy in mind. I wanted to make the differences between public/private spaces more obvious and lighthearted, keeping the intended audience broad and for many ages.

Environmental Design

The environment was the most important aspect of the game in the design process because the game is focused on public/private spaces as well as the fact that exploration is integral in the gameplay. I first started by organizing the differences, so I could move forward with the environmental design.

I created a map for the player to mentally note where they have explored. I wanted to keep the map as playful as the game itself, so I integrated colorful wood blocks into the design. This also creates more memorable monuments, which guide the player around the map.

Playtest Notes

This game did not have a traditional playtest experience because I was presenting to my class as a critique. I had a classmate play the game while I talked about it and occasionally jumped in to focus on a specific interaction.

However, the overall experience was incredibly playful! The viewers and my playtester had a blast exploring and having wacky conversations with the babies. The baby flea market was everyone's favorite part of the map because it was so eccentric. I think focusing on the exploration and environment was a good decision on my part because the class enjoyed the game and wanted a larger map and more interactions. I would say it was a very successful prototype!

Challenges

The biggest challenge for me was prioritizing certain aspects because I initially wanted to have experiences like meeting a boss baby in the office, doing graffiti with a baby on the side of the school, choosing a baby toy, and getting a fortune from a divination table, and more. However, I knew I had a short timeline for the level of detail I wanted to include in the environment.
